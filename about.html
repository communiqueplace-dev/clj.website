About C.L Khanna Jewellers, Amritsar

Twenty-five years of gold, diamond & polki jewellery on Lawrence Road, Amritsar.

Est. 2001 · Amritsar

The Craft of Perfection

In the heart of Amritsar — a city that has adorned brides for centuries — C.L Khanna Jewellers began with a single conviction: that a piece of jewellery is never merely worn. It is inherited, remembered, and retold.

For twenty-five years, our karigars have shaped gold, set uncut polki and matched stones by hand, piece by piece. Every creation that leaves our store is BIS hallmarked and made to the same exacting standard — the craft of perfection that gives our house its name.

What began as a modest counter on Lawrence Road has grown into a name that families return to across generations — daughters now choosing their bridal sets where their mothers once did. Through it all, the workshop behind the showroom has stayed the same: real karigars, real hands, no shortcuts.

From a bride's first haram to a family's everyday gold, we consider it a privilege to be part of your story.

Our Heritage

Rooted in Amritsar

Amritsar has dressed its brides in gold for as long as anyone can remember, and our family has been part of that tradition for a quarter of a century. We grew up around the weighing scale and the polishing wheel, learning that trust is the only thing in this trade that compounds. Every haram, every kada, every nath that leaves our store carries that inheritance.

Our Craft

Made by Hand, Not by Machine

Our karigars still set each polki and matched stone by hand. Designs are drawn with the customer, weighed openly, and priced at the day's rate before any work begins — so there are no surprises, only the piece you imagined. From a single name pendant to a full bridal set, the standard never changes.

Our Promise

Honest Gold, Always

Every creation is BIS hallmarked. Weights are true, making charges are fair, and old jewellery can be redesigned into something new without losing its meaning. We would rather you leave with the right piece than the most expensive one — that is how families have stayed with us for twenty-five years.

Craftsmanship & Quality

Made to Last, Meant to Be Passed On

Every piece that leaves our workshop is shaped by master karigars who set each polki and matched stone entirely by hand. Each creation is BIS hallmarked, weighed openly, and priced at fair making charges before a stroke of work begins — honest gold, no shortcuts.
